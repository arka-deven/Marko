## 1. Campaign Overview

**Campaign Name:** Run 30 Experiments at Once

**One-sentence summary:**
Marko is the AI growth engine that removes the execution bottleneck — generating, validating, running, and killing experiments automatically, so growth teams run 30x more experiments than they could manually.

**Primary Objective:**
Generate 500 signups (free or paid trial) within 8 weeks of MVP launch, with a 15% conversion to paid within 30 days.

**Secondary Objectives:**
- Establish Marko as the category-defining "AI Growth Engine" (not just another experiment tool)
- Build a waitlist of 200+ growth leads before paid channels activate
- Publish 20+ SEO-optimised pieces driving long-tail search traffic

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary — Growth Operators at SaaS Companies
> Marketing managers, heads of growth, and founding teams at B2B SaaS companies (10–200 employees) who are running experiments manually and struggling with execution bandwidth, not ideas.

**Pain point (direct from Cialdini memo):** *"Good growth ideas exist — the bottleneck is the time and headspace required to validate, plan, and run them in parallel."*

**Where they are:** LinkedIn, Product Hunt, Indie Hackers, Twitter/X, growth-specific Slack groups, podcasts (Lenny's Podcast, Growth Unhinged, My First Million)

**Buying stage:** Problem-aware, solution-seeking — they know they need to run more experiments, they don't know a system like Marko exists yet

### Secondary — Consultants and Agencies Running Growth for Clients
> Growth consultants and boutique agencies who manage experiments across multiple clients and need to scale output without scaling headcount.

**Hook:** "Run 30 experiments for each client simultaneously. Bill for strategy, automate the execution."

### Tertiary — Founders Doing Their Own Marketing
> Early-stage founders (seed to Series A) wearing the growth hat, who have ideas but no bandwidth to execute them systematically.

**Hook:** "Your growth instincts are right. Marko makes sure they actually get tested."

## 4. Channel Strategy

### Channel 1 — LinkedIn (Primary Organic)
**Why:** Growth operators live on LinkedIn. Thought leadership drives inbound without paid spend.

**Format:**
- Weekly "Experiment Teardown" posts: take a real experiment (email subject line, pricing page CTA, onboarding flow), show the hypothesis, result, and what to test next
- Short Marko demo clips (15–30s screen recordings showing ideas → launched experiment in < 2 minutes)
- Carousel posts: "7 experiments your competitors are running right now in [channel]"
- Founder posts building in public: "We used Marko to generate 47 experiment ideas this week. Here are the 5 we're actually running."

**Cadence:** 4–5 posts/week
**Effort:** Medium
**Goal:** 500+ followers, 3–5 inbound demo requests/week by Week 6

---

### Channel 2 — SEO / Blog ("The Experiment Brief")
**Why:** Long-tail growth experiment keywords have low competition and high intent.

**Target keywords:**
- "marketing experiment ideas" (1.2k/mo, low KD)
- "A/B test ideas for SaaS" (800/mo, low KD)
- "growth experiment framework" (600/mo, low KD)
- "how to run growth experiments" (1.4k/mo, medium KD)
- "Cialdini principles marketing examples" (2.4k/mo, medium KD)

**Format:** 800–1,200 words, structured as: Hypothesis → Method → Result → What to Test Next. Every post ends with a free Marko CTA.

**Cadence:** 2 posts/week
**Effort:** Medium (content engine produces these)
**Goal:** 1,000 organic sessions/month by Week 12

---

### Channel 3 — Email ("The Weekly Brief")
**Why:** Owned channel, direct relationship, highest conversion rate for SaaS signups.

**Sequences:**

*Pre-launch waitlist nurture (Weeks 1–4):*
- Email 1: "You're on the list. Here's why the best growth teams run 30+ experiments at once."
- Email 2: "The 8-stage experiment pipeline we built for a 5,000-member community" (Cialdini case study)
- Email 3: "The auto-kill rule that frees budget for your winners"
- Email 4: "Your early access is ready."

*Post-signup onboarding (Weeks 5–8):*
- Day 1: Generate your first 10 experiment ideas
- Day 3: Your first experiment is live — here's what to watch
- Day 7: Your first week. What the data says.
- Day 14: Upgrade prompt — "You've run 5 experiments. Growth plan removes all limits."

*Weekly newsletter ("The Experiment Brief"):*
- 1 experiment idea for the week with hypothesis, copy, and expected lift
- Sent Tuesday 9 AM (highest open rate per Cialdini email playbook data)

**Cadence:** Weekly newsletter + triggered onboarding
**Effort:** Low (template-driven)
**Goal:** 40% open rate, 8% CTR, 20% free-to-paid conversion

**Why:** Single highest-density concentration of early adopters and growth practitioners. Top 5 = 500–2,000 signups in 24 hours.

**Effort:** High (one-time)
**Goal:** Top 5 Product of the Day, 300+ upvotes, 500+ signups

**Starting budget:** $500/month hard cap, scale only from ROI-positive experiments
**Effort:** Low to start (boost existing organic)

## Appendix: Marko's Agent Instructions for Marketing

When the AI ideas engine generates marketing experiment ideas for Marko itself, it should draw from this strategic context:

- **Core positioning:** Execution engine, not ideation tool
- **Primary persona:** Growth operator at 10–200 person SaaS company
- **Primary channel:** LinkedIn + Email (organic first, paid only after 15% conversion proven)
- **Forbidden experiments:** Anything that requires > 2 weeks to see results pre-launch. Bias toward experiments measurable in 7 days.
- **Cialdini principle priority:** Social Proof > Authority > Reciprocity for pre-launch. Commitment + Scarcity for conversion.
- **Tone:** Direct, data-backed, practitioner-to-practitioner. Never corporate. Never hype.
- **Claim standard:** No claim without a number or a mechanism behind it. "30x more experiments" = backed by the pipeline's ability to run 20–30 simultaneously.
