## The Master Algorithm: The Atomic Content Flywheel

Everything starts with one anchor piece per week. That anchor is repurposed into every channel below. This is not cross-posting — it is native reformatting. The same idea, rebuilt for each platform's specific algorithm.

```
ANCHOR PIECE (created once per week)
        ↓
┌────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  LinkedIn post (long-form, text + insight)  │
│  Blog post (SEO-optimised, 1,000 words)     │
│  Video script (60–90 sec, one hook)         │
└────────────────────────────────────────────┘
        ↓
DERIVATIVES (created from the anchor)
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ TikTok video         │ Instagram Reel   │ YT Short    │
│ Instagram post       │ Instagram Story  │ YT Long     │
│ X/Twitter thread     │ Email newsletter │ LinkedIn carousel │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Why this works:** Content flywheel teams generate 4x more leads per dollar than teams creating from scratch per channel. Repurposed content gets 25–35% more engagement than one-off posts because it reinforces the message across every surface the audience uses.

**Production rule:** Create the anchor on Monday. Derivatives go out Tuesday–Friday. One week's cycle, one idea fully exhausted.

## Platform 1: LinkedIn

### Why it's the primary channel
Growth operators at B2B SaaS companies (Marko's core audience) are more active on LinkedIn than any other platform. LinkedIn thought leadership generates 277% more leads than other platforms for B2B SaaS.

### How the algorithm works (2026)
- **Employee posts outperform company pages 6–8x in reach.** Post as the founder, not the company account.
- **Average post reach: 8–12% of followers** (down from 15–20%). Beat this with comment velocity.
- **Dwell time + meaningful comments** matter more than likes. A back-and-forth reply chain extends a post's reach for hours.
- **Educational content gets 3–5x more reach** than personal updates or promotional posts.
- **Carousels have the highest engagement rate (6.60%).** Use for "7 experiments your team should run" style posts.
- **Video is being heavily boosted** — LinkedIn is pushing video to compete with TikTok.

### Content types and cadence

| Format | Frequency | Algorithm goal |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Text post (thought leadership) | 3x/week | Dwell time + comments |
| Carousel (slide deck post) | 1x/week | Saves + reshares |
| Native video (60–90 sec) | 1x/week | Watch time + DM sends |
| Poll | 1x/2 weeks | Engagement velocity |

### What to post — Marko-specific angles

**Text posts (Mon, Wed, Fri):**
- "We ran 30 experiments last month. 27 failed. Here's what the 3 winners had in common."
- "The experiment you should stop running right now (and why it will never show lift)"
- "Your growth team doesn't have an ideas problem. They have a _____ problem." (fill-in-the-blank = engagement trigger)
- Build-in-public numbers: "Week 6 update: X signups, Y experiments running, Z winners"
- Cialdini principle applied to a real growth story (one per week)

**Carousels (Thursday):**
- "7 SaaS experiments that work on every channel" (7 slides, one per experiment)
- "The 8-stage growth pipeline we built for a 5,000-member community"
- "How to write a growth experiment brief in 5 minutes" (step-by-step)

**Native video (Tuesday):**
- Screen recording: idea generated in Marko → experiment live in under 2 minutes
- Talk-to-camera: "Why we auto-kill experiments that run longer than 30 days"

### Key metric to optimise
**Comments per post.** A post with 20+ comments will get pushed to 2nd-degree networks organically. End every post with a direct question to trigger this.

## Platform 2: X / Twitter

### How the algorithm works (2026)
- **Reply is worth 27x a like. A reply chain is worth 150x a like.** Build posts that demand a response.
- **First 30–60 minutes is everything.** Engagement velocity in the first hour determines total reach.
- **External links are suppressed** since March 2026. Non-Premium accounts posting links get near-zero reach. Put links in the first reply, not the post body.
- **X Premium** subscribers get 4–8x the organic reach. Worth the subscription for the founder account.
- **Text beats video** on X by 30% — the only platform where this is true.
- Best posting times: **weekday mornings, 7–10 AM** for B2B topics.

### Content types and cadence

| Format | Frequency | Note |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Thread (5–10 tweets) | 3x/week | Link in reply #1 |
| Single opinion post | 2x/week | Contrarian + question at end |
| Reply to trending growth content | Daily | Builds visibility in the growth community |
| Repost of growth practitioners | 2x/week | Reciprocity = they'll return the favour |

### What to post — Marko-specific angles

**Threads:**
- "We analysed 200 growth experiments. Here's what the winners had in common: [thread]"
- "The Cialdini principle most SaaS teams ignore (and how to use it in your next experiment)"
- "Auto-kill rule: if your experiment hasn't moved the needle in 30 days, it's already dead. Here's how to set it up [thread]"

**Single posts:**
- Hot takes: "Most A/B tests are theatre. Change my mind."
- Data drops: "47 experiment ideas generated in 8 seconds. This is what Marko does every Monday morning."
- Questions: "What's the most surprising growth experiment result you've seen? Drop it below."

**Daily replies:**
- Reply to Lenny Rachitsky, Kyle Poyar, Elena Verna, Andrew Chen, Brian Balfour posts with a specific insight — not "great post"

### Key metric to optimise
**Replies received per post.** Track which post types generate reply chains and double down on those.

## Platform 3: TikTok

### How the algorithm works (2026)
- **Saves are the highest-intent signal.** Educational content that people save to reference later gets pushed to new audiences aggressively.
- **Engagement velocity in the first hour** determines FYP distribution. The first 60 minutes after posting is the make-or-break window.
- **Watch time over follower count.** A new account with zero followers can reach 100k people if the video holds attention.
- **Sweet spot: 60–90 seconds** for branded organic content. 15–30 seconds for ads.
- **Native posting beats scheduling tools by 20–50%** in FYP distribution.
- **Quality > frequency.** One great video per week beats 7 mediocre ones.

### Content types and cadence

| Format | Frequency | Length |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Educational hook video | 2x/week | 60–90 sec |
| "Day in the life" founder | 1x/2 weeks | 30–60 sec |
| Experiment teardown | 1x/week | 90 sec |
| Trend-hijack (growth topic) | Opportunistic | 15–30 sec |

### What to post — Marko-specific angles

**Hook structure for every video:**
```
Hook (0–3 sec): Bold claim or question
Build (3–45 sec): Deliver the insight fast, no filler
Reveal (45–75 sec): The tool/system/result
CTA (last 5 sec): "Save this" or "What would you test first?"
```

**Video ideas:**
- "The reason your A/B test is lying to you" (save-bait educational)
- "We ran 30 growth experiments simultaneously. Here's how." (screen demo)
- "The auto-kill rule that saves growth teams thousands" (practical tip)
- "How one experiment idea turned into $40k in attributed revenue" (case study)
- "I put 47 experiment ideas into an AI. Here's what it gave me." (screen recording)

**Key rule:** Never mention "sign up" or "try our product" in the video. The CTA is always to save the video or follow for more. Awareness only — conversion happens off-platform.

### Key metric to optimise
**Saves per 1,000 views.** If saves are above 3%, the video is being pushed. If below 1%, the hook or content quality is the problem.

## Platform 4: Instagram — Feed Posts (Static Images)

### How the algorithm works (2026)
- **Sends via DM are the top signal** for feed and Reels recommendation to non-followers.
- **Original content only** — accounts that post 10+ reposts in 30 days are removed from Explore entirely.
- **Relationship strength matters more** for feed than for Reels. Your followers' engagement history with you determines if they see your post.
- **Carousels get more swipes** = more time spent = higher algorithmic priority.
- **Views is now the universal metric** across all formats.

### Content types and cadence

| Format | Frequency | Goal |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Carousel (knowledge post) | 2x/week | Saves + sends |
| Single graphic (data/stat) | 1x/week | Shares to stories |
| Infographic (experiment framework) | 1x/2 weeks | Saves + Explore |

### What to post — Marko-specific angles

**Carousel ideas:**
- "7 growth experiments by channel" (1 per slide, clean visual)
- "The 8-stage growth pipeline" (diagram on each slide)
- "Cialdini's 7 principles applied to SaaS marketing" (one principle per slide)
- "Auto-kill checklist: is your experiment ready to be shut down?"

**Single graphics:**
- Stats card: "Teams that run 30+ experiments per month grow 2.7x faster than those running 5"
- Quote card: "You don't have an ideas problem. You have an execution problem."
- Experiment result card: "[Channel] experiment lifted conversion by X% in 14 days"

**Design system:**
- Black background (#0f0f0f), lime accent (#a3e635), white text
- Bold sans-serif numbers (stat-forward)
- Always include Marko wordmark, never the URL (algorithm suppresses)

### Key metric to optimise
**Sends (DM shares) per post.** Instagram tells creators this directly in insights now. If a post gets shared via DM, Instagram pushes it to new audiences. Design every carousel to be share-worthy ("send this to your growth team").

## Platform 5: Instagram — Reels

### How the algorithm works (2026)
- **Reels can now be up to 20 minutes.** Longer storytelling content (tutorials, mini-docs) is now being recommended in Explore if it holds watch time.
- **Sends via DM is the #1 signal** — even more weighted for Reels than feed.
- **Watch-through rate** is critical. If people swipe off in the first 3 seconds, the Reel dies.
- **Original audio or trending audio** — trending audio multiplies reach by 2–4x when combined with relevant content.

### Content types and cadence

| Format | Length | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Repurposed TikTok (no watermark) | 60–90 sec | 2x/week |
| Tutorial Reel (product walkthrough) | 2–5 min | 1x/week |
| Talking head insight | 30–60 sec | 1x/week |

### What to post — Marko-specific angles

**Hook structure (same as TikTok, native-posted without watermark):**
- Remove TikTok watermark before uploading (watermarks suppress Instagram distribution)
- Re-record audio if the TikTok used native sounds not on Instagram

**Longer Reels (2–5 min) — Instagram-exclusive:**
- "Full walkthrough: generating 10 experiment ideas in Marko and launching one in 2 minutes"
- "Behind the build: how we designed the auto-kill algorithm"
- "Interview: how a growth consultant runs 30 experiments for 5 clients simultaneously"

### Key metric to optimise
**Watch time %** for the first 3 seconds (hook retention) and **sends per view**.

## Platform 7: YouTube — Long-Form

### How the algorithm works (2026)
- **Watch time and average view duration** are the primary long-form signals. YouTube values long-term viewer satisfaction over short-term spikes.
- **Click-through rate (CTR)** from the thumbnail is the gating signal — if people don't click, the video never gets watch time.
- **Session starts** — videos that bring people onto YouTube (from email, social) are boosted.
- **Subscriber satisfaction** over time: YouTube uses direct surveys on "Did you enjoy this video?" to calibrate reach.
- Long-form is where **buying decisions are influenced** for B2B. 10-minute walkthroughs convert better than any short.

### Content types and cadence

| Format | Length | Frequency |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Tutorial / walkthrough | 8–15 min | 2x/month |
| Case study deep-dive | 12–20 min | 1x/month |
| Interview (growth practitioner) | 25–40 min | 1x/month |

### What to post — Marko-specific angles
- "How to run 30 growth experiments simultaneously (full walkthrough)"
- "We analysed 200 SaaS experiments: what separates winners from losers"
- "The Cialdini Growth System: how we built an AI that runs experiments using psychology"
- "Growth operator interviews: [Guest Name] on running systematic experiments at [Company]"

**Thumbnail rule:** Bold number or contrarian claim. White/lime on black. Face if possible.
**Title rule:** Number + strong outcome. "30 Experiments in 30 Days: What We Learned"

### Key metric to optimise
**Average view duration (%)** — aim for >50% average. If people drop off before 50%, the content is too slow or the hook is wrong. Fix the first 60 seconds first.

## Platform 8: YouTube — Shorts

### How the algorithm works (2026)
- **Swipe-away rate** — the opposite of watch time. If people swipe past in the first second, the Short is dead.
- **Watch-through rate** — what % watch to the end.
- **Replay rate** — people rewatching is a strong positive signal.
- Shorts reach **different audiences** than long-form on the same channel. Use Shorts to grow subscribers who then watch long-form.
- **3–5 Shorts per week** is the growth cadence. Extract every Short from a long-form video.

### Content types and cadence

| Format | Frequency | Source |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Extracted clips from long-form | 3–5x/week | Long-form video above |
| Repurposed TikTok (no watermark) | 2–3x/week | TikTok content |
| Standalone quick tips | 1x/week | Original |

### Key metric to optimise
**Subscriber conversion from Shorts** — how many Short viewers click "Subscribe" after watching. Aim for 2%+.

## Platform 9: Email — The Experiment Brief (Newsletter)

### How the algorithm works
Email has no algorithm. It is your most powerful owned channel. Every subscriber is a direct, guaranteed reach. Priority: **grow this list above all other channels**.

### Sequences

**Pre-launch waitlist (4 emails):**
1. "You're on the list — here's the execution bottleneck killing growth teams"
2. "The 8-stage experiment pipeline we built for a 5,000-member community"
3. "The auto-kill rule that frees your budget for winners"
4. "Your early access is ready"

**Post-signup onboarding (5 emails):**
- Day 1: "Generate your first 10 experiment ideas"
- Day 3: "Your first experiment is live — here's what to watch"
- Day 7: "Week one. What the data says."
- Day 14: Upgrade prompt
- Day 30: Re-engagement ("You've run X experiments. Growth plan removes all limits.")

**Weekly newsletter — The Experiment Brief (every Tuesday 9 AM):**

Structure (reusable template):
```
Subject: [One experiment idea] — The Experiment Brief #[N]

1. THE EXPERIMENT (3 sentences)
   Hypothesis: [what you're testing]
   Method: [how to run it, what to change]
   Expected lift: [X–Y%]

2. ONE THING WE LEARNED THIS WEEK
   [From Marko's own data or a user result]

3. THE CIALDINI PLAY
   [One Cialdini principle + how to apply it this week]

CTA: "Try this experiment in Marko →" [link]
```

### Key metrics to optimise
- **Open rate:** Target 40%+ (industry avg 21%). Achieved by: list hygiene, consistent send day, strong subject lines.
- **CTR:** Target 8%+. Achieved by: one clear CTA per email, no link overload.
- **Reply rate:** Track replies as the strongest engagement signal. End every newsletter with a direct question.

## Platform 10: SEO Blog — "The Experiment Brief"

### How the algorithm works (Google 2026)
- **Helpful Content system** — content must be written for people, not search engines. Thin content is demoted algorithmically.
- **E-E-A-T** (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust) — author attribution matters. Byline every post with a real name.
- **Long-tail, low-competition keywords** are the fastest path to rank. Target KD < 20 first.
- **Internal linking** distributes authority across posts. Link every new post to 2–3 existing posts.
- **Time-to-rank:** Expect 6–12 weeks for new domain, 2–4 weeks for established. SEO is a compounding channel — start now even if results come later.

### Target keywords (prioritised)

| Keyword | Volume | KD | Priority |
|---------|--------|-----|----------|
| marketing experiment ideas | 1,200/mo | Low | Must |
| A/B test ideas for SaaS | 800/mo | Low | Must |
| growth experiment framework | 600/mo | Low | Must |
| how to run growth experiments | 1,400/mo | Medium | High |
| Cialdini principles marketing examples | 2,400/mo | Medium | High |
| experiment hypothesis template | 400/mo | Low | High |
| auto-kill experiment rule | 200/mo | Low | Quick win |

### Post format (every post follows this structure)

```
Title: [Keyword-rich, 50–60 chars]
Intro: Hook in 2 sentences. What they'll learn. No fluff.

H2: The Hypothesis
H2: How to Run It
H2: What to Measure
H2: Real Results (or expected results)
H2: What to Test Next

CTA: Free Marko trial (last paragraph)
Word count: 900–1,200 words
Author: [Founder name]
Internal links: 2–3 to related posts
```

### Cadence
2 posts per week. Use Tuesday (after newsletter) and Friday. Repurpose the newsletter's experiment idea as the blog post — same idea, SEO-optimised format.

### Key metric to optimise
**Organic impressions in Google Search Console.** Clicks come after impressions establish. In the first 3 months, impressions growing weekly is the leading indicator that the content is working.

## Content Pillar System (Rotate Weekly)

To avoid burnout and algorithm fatigue, rotate through 4 content pillars:

| Week | Pillar | Anchor idea |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Week 1 | **Experiment Teardown** | Real or hypothetical experiment: hypothesis → result → what to test next |
| Week 2 | **The Execution Problem** | Why growth teams fail at running experiments, not generating ideas |
| Week 3 | **Cialdini Deep Dive** | One principle, applied to a specific growth channel with examples |
| Week 4 | **Build in Public** | Real Marko data: signups, experiments running, what worked, what didn't |

## Marko's Own Content Engine (Dogfooding)

Marko generates experiment ideas for growth teams. Marko should also run experiments on its own marketing using itself.

Each month, run these experiments through Marko:
- Email subject line A/B (The Experiment Brief open rate)
- LinkedIn post format (text vs. carousel vs. video)
- CTA placement in blog post (above fold vs. end of post)
- Instagram Reel hook wording

Track results in Marko dashboard. The best-performing experiments become content: "We ran this experiment on our own marketing. Here's what we learned."
